History of Netrokona University

Established to provide quality higher education and research opportunities in the Mymensingh division of Bangladesh.

Establishment & Foundation

Netrokona University was established in 2018 as a government-funded public university. Located in the Netrokona district of Mymensingh Division, it was founded to expand access to higher education outside Dhaka and to foster regional academic growth. The first Vice-Chancellor was Prof. Dr. Rafique Ullah Khan.

Early Development

The university began its journey in March 2019 with a small number of students and limited departments, including Bangla, English, Economics, and Computer Science & Engineering. Despite being new, it quickly became known for its focus on academic excellence and its commitment to serving the local community.

Growth & Expansion

Since its establishment, Netrokona University has been expanding its academic programs and campus facilities. Plans are underway to develop a permanent campus with modern academic buildings, student dormitories, and research centers. The university is steadily growing both in student enrollment and in its role as a hub of learning and innovation for the region.
